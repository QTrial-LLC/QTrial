## Priority 1 - unblocks the next piece of work

These are the questions where the answer directly shapes what we build next. Each should take less than five minutes to answer.

### 1. Paper-entry handling

When a paper entry arrives in the mail:

- Do you keep the physical paper after entering it into Obedience Solution? If yes, how long? In what kind of folder or filing system?
- Do you staple the check to the entry form, or keep them separate?
- If the entry form is incomplete (missing signature, missing JH number, wrong fee), do you call/email the exhibitor, fix it yourself, or return the whole thing?

### 2. Judge communication

How do you currently send judges their assignments and judges' books?

- Email the PDFs? Mail printed copies? Hand them to the judge on Friday at setup?
- Does the judge receive the judges' book before the trial, or only at the trial itself?
- Do you send any pre-trial paperwork to judges besides the book (schedule, contact sheet for exhibitors with special accommodations)?

### 3. Refund by check

When you need to refund a check payment (someone paid by check, then withdrew):

- Do you hand-write a refund check from the club's checkbook?
- Does the club use a bill-pay service or online banking feature for refunds?
- Do you batch refunds or issue them as needed?

### 4. Waitlist payment policy

When a dog goes on the waitlist because the class is full:

- Does the exhibitor's card get charged immediately (and refunded if they don't promote off the waitlist)?
- Is the card authorized but not charged (hold that expires if not promoted)?
- Or is no payment collected until they're promoted into the trial?

Also: historically, does Glens Falls typically have a waitlist? Are the limits usually hit?

### 5. "Ring 0" question

In your Obedience Solution judging schedule and judges' books, all the classes say "Ring #0" or "Ring 0." Glens Falls is clearly running one ring. Is "Ring 0" a deliberate convention, or is it just whatever Obedience Solution defaults to when you don't set a ring number? (I suspect the latter, but I want to make sure OffLeash doesn't replicate a confusing default.)

### 6. "Transfer to X" dropdown usage

When you're entering an exhibitor's entry in Obedience Solution and you see the class dropdown with options like:

```
Rally Novice A
Transfer to Rally Novice B
Rally Novice B
Transfer to Rally Intermediate
```

Do you:

- (a) Pick "Transfer to Rally Novice B" at entry time because the exhibitor told you "enter Novice A, and if my dog earns the RN before the trial, move me to Novice B"?
- (b) Only use those "Transfer to" options after the fact, when the exhibitor has actually submitted a post-closing transfer request?
- (c) Both, depending on the situation?

(This tells us whether OffLeash should offer transfer-intent at entry time, only after-the-fact, or both.)

### 7. The "glitchy" AKC form

When you said the AKC online form "wouldn't do the totals plus there was a thing about dual classes":

- What browser and PDF reader were you using? (Adobe Reader vs browser-built-in vs Chrome)
- What do you mean by "dual classes"? Were you trying to enter both an Obedience and a Rally trial on one form, or something else?
- Is this the online fillable JOVOB7 / JOVRY8, or a separate AKC web portal?

If you remember the URL or can screenshot the form where it broke, that's gold.
